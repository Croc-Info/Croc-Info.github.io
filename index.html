The Saltwater Crocodile

Crocodylus porosus: The King of Estuaries

Overview

The Saltwater Crocodile is the largest of all living reptiles and the dominant predator in its environment, found in coastal waters, estuaries, and rivers from India to Australia [10].

Key Facts

  • Size: Males can reach up to 6-7 meters (20-23 ft) in length and weigh over 1,000 kg.
  • Habitat: Highly adaptable, inhabiting saltwater, mangrove swamps, and sometimes far inland [10].
  • Diet: Apex predator, consuming anything from fish and birds to sharks and buffalo.
  • Status: Generally classified as Least Concern, but conservation status varies by region [10].

Behavior

Saltwater crocodiles are known for their territorial behavior and dangerous nature to humans [10]. They are patient hunters, using stealth and immense biting force to secure prey.
